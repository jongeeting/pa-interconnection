Pennsylvania's Generation Pipeline Is Stuck Behind a Federal Tax Cliff

70 Pennsylvania generation projects — 1,583 MW of summer-peak capacity — have already cleared PJM's engineering review and executed Generation Interconnection Agreements. They are stuck on state and local permitting. For the solar and wind subset, the One Big Beautiful Bill Act's federal tax credit deadlines threaten to void project economics within 14 months. Governor Shapiro's HB 502 is the obvious vehicle, but its 25 MW eligibility threshold excludes most of the projects at risk. This is the case for lowering it to 10 MW — or lower.

Explore the map → Download the data
Pennsylvania's active interconnection queue is overwhelmingly clean energy. Solar, storage, and solar+storage hybrids together account for 84% of the 10,400 MW pipeline. Natural gas is 4%. Whether Pennsylvania captures or forfeits this generation depends on Harrisburg. — Build Philly Now analysis of PJM Planning Queues data

The case in briefPennsylvania has a permitting bottleneck and a federal countdown clock.

The bottleneck is what makes the policy necessary. The cliff is what makes the timing urgent. HB 502, with the right amendments, addresses both.

1.

The pipeline is real, vetted, and stuck

PJM has already determined these 70 projects can be safely interconnected and signed agreements with each developer. They are not waiting on engineering. They are waiting on people. Most have been in development since 2018–2020.

2.

The federal cliff hits in 14 months

Under OBBBA, solar and wind projects must "begin construction" — meet IRS Notice 2025-42's Physical Work Test — by July 4, 2026, or be fully placed in service by year-end 2027. State and local permits are the binding prerequisite.

3.

HB 502's threshold misses the target

The bill's 25 MW eligibility floor was set as a generic "utility-scale" default. PA's pipeline is overwhelmingly community-scale. Lowering the threshold to 10 MW would expand HB 502's coverage of stuck projects from 29% to 83%.

The 10 MW line is also defensible on first principles. Below 10 MW, PA solar projects typically operate as net-metered or distributed generation and fall under different regulatory frameworks. Above 10 MW, projects are unambiguously commercial-scale and reasonably subject to a state siting standard. The line is principled, not just convenient.

MethodologyHow this analysis works

Source data

PJM Planning Queues file (legacy queue snapshot, pre-Cycle 1 reform). Obtained via researchers at the University of Pennsylvania ENVS 5100 group through RMI. PA-only export with 1,976 historical and active projects across all fuel types.

Active pipeline definition

Excludes Withdrawn, In Service, and Deactivated projects. Includes Active, Engineering and Procurement, Suspended, Under Construction, and Partially in Service — 340 PA projects, 10,421 MW summer-peak capacity.

GIA-posted urgent universe

Projects whose Generation Interconnection Agreement column shows "Document Posted" — i.e., the agreement has been executed and posted publicly by PJM. 70 PA projects, 1,583 MW. This is the population whose engineering review is complete and whose binding constraint is now state and local permitting.

Federal cliff exposure

Solar and wind projects only. Under OBBBA, these technologies must "begin construction" by July 4, 2026 (per IRS Notice 2025-42's Physical Work Test) or be placed in service by December 31, 2027. Standalone storage and nuclear are on the original IRA timeline through 2033 and are not directly cliff-exposed.

HB 502 threshold

Bill text uses "capacity," interpreted here as PJM's "MW Capacity" (summer net) field. For solar projects this typically runs lower than "MW Energy" (winter net), so the threshold semantics matter. Confirm with bill drafters before final use.

Senator attribution

County-level approximation based on PA State Senate districts post-2022 redistricting. Many counties span multiple districts; the dominant senator is shown. Project locations shown at jittered county centroids since precise lat/lon was not available in the source file.

Limitations. This analysis covers the legacy PJM queue, which closed before April 2026. PJM's Cycle 1 — 811 projects, 220 GW system-wide — will produce a state-level breakdown in coming weeks. When that data lands, we will refresh this analysis to include both the legacy and Cycle 1 PA pipelines. The "stuck on permitting" inference for suspended projects is not yet directly verified; project-level outreach or FOIL requests would confirm. Senator names and district numbers are BPN to verify.
